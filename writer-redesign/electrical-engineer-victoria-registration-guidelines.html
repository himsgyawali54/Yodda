Electrical Engineers victoria registration guidelines 2023

Are you an electrical or electronics engineer looking to work in Victoria, Australia? Do you know the guidelines for 2023 Victorian registration for electrical and electronics engineers? You don’t have to worry we got you covered.

To be registered as an electrical engineer in Victoria, you must undergo a competency assessment process to ensure that your qualifications and experience meet the standards required for registration. This process is managed by the Victorian Building Authority (VBA) and involves applying for assessment to Engineers Australia, who will assess your qualifications and experience.

In this article, we will discuss the steps involved in getting assessed for Victorian registration for electrical engineers so that you can confidently navigate the process and achieve your career goals.

Who Needs To Be Assessed?

Electrical and electronics engineers seeking to register with the Business Licensing Authority (BLA) in Victoria, Australia, must first undergo a competency assessment. This assessment is also required for engineers living outside Victoria but working on projects within the state.

To meet the 1 June deadline, electrical and electronics engineers must apply for a competency assessment by March 2023 at the latest. This assessment is critical to ensure that their qualifications and experience meet the required standards for registration as an engineer in Victoria and to enable them to deliver professional engineering services in these areas.

A. Assessment Options for electronics engineers in Victoria

1. Competence assessment

To become a registered professional engineer in Victoria, you must undergo a competence assessment. This can be done through one of the available pathways, and the outcome report must be provided to the Building Licensing Authority (BLA).

2. Building endorsement assessment

You must also undergo a building endorsement assessment to practice in the Victorian building industry.

B. Assessment pathways offered by Engineers Australia

1. Simple assessment pathways

Engineers Australia provides multiple straightforward assessment pathways for engineers seeking registration. It is unnecessary to be a member of the organization to utilize these pathways.

C. Victorian Engineering Registration Requirements For Electrical Engineers

The process for registering as a professional engineer in Victoria, Australia, involves two steps:

Step 1: Qualification and experience assessment

  1. Choose an assessment entity approved by the Business Licensing Authority (BLA) and apply to have your qualifications and experience or competencies assessed.
  2. After the assessment, the assessment entity will provide a report on the areas of engineering in which you are eligible to apply for registration and endorsement.
  3. If you are deemed to be a registered and endorsed building engineer, you have until 30 June 2026 to have your qualifications and experience assessed.
  4. You only need to complete this step if you are a civil aviation safety engineer.

Step 2: Apply for registration and endorsement

  1. Lodge an application for registration and endorsement with the BLA through myCAV, including the report from the assessment entity.
  2. The BLA will assess your application and check your report to determine eligibility.
  3. Suppose you have applied for endorsement to work in the building industry. In that case, the BLA will refer your application to the Victorian Building Authority for a report on whether you are a fit and proper person under the Building Act 1993.
  4. You can apply for registration as a practicing or non-practicing professional engineer.
  5. The registration and endorsement duration is three years from the grant date.

D. Know About Your BLA Assessment

  1. You need an Engineers Australia Identification (EA ID) to apply for the BLA-Direct assessment.
  2. You should complete an application via the State Registration Eligibility tile on MyPortal to submit your BLA direct application.
  3. The first component of the assessment is an Entry to Practice assessment of your qualifications.
  4. If you have a qualification accredited under the Washington Accord, your Entry to Practice competency will be confirmed. Your application will move to the second component, the Independent Practice assessment stage.
  5. If you have a qualification not accredited under the Washington Accord, you must undertake an Eligibility for Membership (Stage 1) assessment.
  6. The second assessment component is the Independent Practice assessment, where you must provide a CV, names, and contact details of two referees and examples of work.
  7. The BLA-Direct Assessor will review your documentation, contact your referees, and arrange an interview to validate your identification, experience, and competency claims.
  8. If you have an appropriate Eligibility for Membership (Stage 1) assessment, Migration Skills Assessment outcome letter, or an accredited Washington Accord qualification, there is no fee for the BLA-Direct assessment's Entry to Practice component.

What Happens After The Assessment Is Successful?

If your assessment is successful, you will be issued a Letter of Assessment, which you can then use to support your application for registration with the VBA. If your assessment is unsuccessful, you may be required to undertake further study or gain additional work experience before reapplying for assessment.

It is important to note that the assessment process for electrical engineers without Chartered or NER credentials may take longer than for those with these credentials, and the requirements for registration may be more rigorous. However, successful registration as an electrical engineer in Victoria can open up various career opportunities and allow you to work on meaningful projects across the state.

Members Without Chartered Or Ner Credentials For Victorian Registration

In Victoria, Australia, electrical engineers who still need to get Chartered or National Engineering Register (NER) credentials can still apply for registration through the Victorian Building Authority (VBA) assessment process.

The assessment process for electrical engineers without Chartered or NER credentials involves applying to Engineers Australia, along with evidence of your qualifications and work experience. Engineers Australia will then assess your skills and knowledge to determine whether you meet the requirements for registration as an electrical engineer in Victoria.
