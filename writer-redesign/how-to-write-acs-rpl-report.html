How to Write ACS RPL Report on Your Own?

  10 minute read

Publish date: 2021/11/1

Listen
  • 7 Steps to write ACS RPL report
  • Don't want to write on your own?

ACS RPL Report is the ultimate answer for you if you lack a formal ICT qualification but want to make a skilled migration to Australia. It is a document submitted to the Australian Computer Society for evaluation. However, writing an RPL is not an easy task; it requires the utmost attention and dedication.

If you have made up your mind and are all set to write the report all by yourself, then you have come to the right place. We will guide you in preparing an impressive ACS RPL report on your own. Let’s get started!

7 Steps to Write ACS RPL Report on Your Own

Follow the below guide for ACS RPL writing:

  • Step 1: Understand the ACS RPL Guidelines
  • Step 2: Pick the Correct Categories in the First Part of Section 1
  • Step 3: Select the Suitable Topics in Second Part of Section 1
  • Step 4: Choose the Projects for Section 2
  • Step 5: Provide Correct Details of the Project
  • Step 6: Strongly Describe Your Roles and Responsibilities
  • Step 7: Read, Edit, and Proofread your ACS RPL Report

Step 1: Understand the ACS RPL Guidelines

The first step is to comprehend the ACS guidelines for RPL and then fill out the ACS RPL form. If you ignore or do not address all the guidelines of ACS, you can face RPL rejection, termination of your application, and even a ban on reapplying RPL. So, you must be fully aware of what, how, and do’s & don’ts of ACS RPL Report.

*Note: The information below requires understanding of the format of RPL report. So, before going any further, check the RPL Report Format.

After understanding the format, continue further.

Step 2: Pick the Correct Categories in the First Part of Section 1

The 'Professionalism as it applies in ICT' is the first part of the Body of ICT Knowledge/Section 1. It includes the general knowledge foundations any ICT professional will have. You can choose any 2 from the following 5 categories:

  • Professional ICT Ethics
  • Impacts of ICT
  • Working Individually and in ICT development teams
  • Professional Communication
  • The Professional ICT Practitioner

Step 3: Select the Suitable Topics in Second Part of Section 1

‘Core ICT Knowledge’ is the second part of section 1. It includes the foundational aspects of Information and Communications Technology. It covers the basic ideas, rules, and parts that create ICT systems. You can choose any 2 topics from the following:

  • ICT Fundamentals
  • ICT Infrastructure
  • Information and Data Science Engineering
  • Computational Science and Engineering
  • Application Systems
  • Cyber Security
  • ICT Projects
  • ICT Management and Governance

Step 4: Choose the Projects for Section 2

The two project reports in section 2 serve as a comprehensive account of an ICT project/ task. These reports showcase your extensive knowledge in a specific area of ICT and practical application of the ICT Body of Knowledge.

In section 2, you must provide details of:

  • Project Summary
  • Business Opportunity or Problem
  • Solution
  • Results

One of your chosen ACS RPL project reports must have been undertaken in the last 3 years and another one within 5 years.

Step 5: Provide Correct Details of the Project

After choosing the ACS RPL project reports that best describe your competence and skills, you must provide some crucial project details. The project detail includes:

  • Client’s Company Name
  • Legal Name of Entity
  • Business Address
  • Street Address
  • Suburb State
  • Postcode Country
  • Contact Numbers (Telephone, Web address, Email Address)
  • Nature of project
  • Location of project
  • Name of your employer
  • Start date
  • End date

Step 6: Strongly Describe Your Roles and Responsibilities

You must clearly state your role and duties for the project. Make sure to:

  • Highlight your achievements
  • Represent yourself to be following ACS Code of Professional Conduct
  • Show you can work best in both individual and group projects
  • Demonstrate your strong base on the theoretical aspects of the relevant field
  • Make the lesson learned section about technical and professional learning

The roles and responsibilities part hints to the RPL assessors about:

  1. your capability and professional ICT skills
  2. your suitability for the nominated ICT occupation

When writing this part, make sure to cover every small to big responsibility you were given in the respective project.

Example:

Do: I successfully contributed to project design, offering creative solutions and technical expertise, which resulted in a 20% increase in performance.

Don’t: I participated in the design team meetings.

Step 7: Read, Edit, and Proofread your ACS RPL Report

Once you've completed your ACS RPL report, you need to carefully read to identify unclear parts, and proofread it thoroughly. With editing and proofreading, you can get rid of RPL Writing Mistakes and make your report clear and free of errors.

Here are some expert tips for proofreading:

  • Make sure your report is in simple Australian Language
  • Always write in small paragraphs
  • Check for AI content: Remove if any
  • Check for Plagiarism: Remove if any

After following these steps in order, your ACS RPL Report will be ready to submit for ACS skills assessment. Best of luck!
