So, what exactly is the KA02 Knowledge Assessment? Let's delve into it.

What is KA02 Knowledge Assessment?

  • Who needs it?
  • Eligibility Criteria
  • Benefits

KA02 knowledge assessment is a competency-based assessment of professional engineers who want to migrate to New Zealand on a skilled visa. This formal assessment evaluates your knowledge and skills in a variety of engineering-related topics as per NZ professional standards.

It is done by professional assessors of Engineering New Zealand (ENZ), formally known as the Institute of Professional Engineers of New Zealand (IPENZ).

Fun fact: Even though the name of the assessing body has changed, many people still refer to the assessment process as “IPENZ KA02 assessment” rather than “Engineering New Zealand KA02 assessment”.

Who needs KA02 Assessment?

Several types of individuals need it:

  • Engineers seeking skilled migration to New Zealand
  • Engineers whose engineering qualification is not from the Washington Accord or any university in New Zealand
  • Engineers pursuing professional registration in New Zealand
  • Engineers who wish to demonstrate expertise for career advancement
  • Individuals whose work experience deviates from traditional pathways

Eligibility criteria for KA02 Knowledge Assessment

  • Education:

    You can apply for KA02 assessment if you do not have a four-year Bachelor's degree from a Washington Accord accredited university or a university in New Zealand.

  • Work Experience:

    You must demonstrate the use of engineering skills in a practical setting i.e., 3-4 engineering projects or activities (during Work/Study Episodes). This experience should showcase your skills and knowledge in applying engineering principles to practical situations.

  • Competency:

    You must showcase a knowledge profile equal to that of a Washington Accord graduate.

Benefits of KA02 Assessment

This assessment offers valuable benefits for both individuals seeking to migrate to New Zealand and the country itself. Here are some key benefits:

For individuals:

  • For skilled migration: A positive KA02 assessment is essential for visa approval and skilled migration.
  • Career validation and recognition: This assessment enhances an engineer's value in the job market, and provides national, international, and multinational job opportunities.

For New Zealand:

  • Skilled workforce: The assessment process ensures that only qualified and competent engineers are entering the New Zealand workforce.
  • Economic benefits: Attracting skilled professionals through skilled migrant visas boosts the country's economy.

Knowledge Assessment Pathways

There are two KA02 knowledge assessment pathways.

  1. Standard Knowledge Assessment
  2. Fast Tracked Knowledge Assessment
Standard Knowledge Assessment Fast Tracked Knowledge Assessment
For Whom? This pathway applies to all engineers seeking assessment, whether for immigration or chartered membership purposes. This pathway applies only to engineers who need knowledge assessment for immigration purposes.

What Documents are Necessary for KA02 Knowledge Assessment?

You need the following documents and information:

  • Academic Transcripts
  • KA02 Report
  • CV
  • Application fee payment proof
  • Additional Supporting Document

1. Academic Transcripts

The academic transcripts are official documents from educational institutions where you earned your qualification. These documents include information about courses you took, grades you got, and overall academic performance in engineering or related fields.

2. KA02 Report

KA02 Report Engineering New Zealand uses this report to assess your competence against the Washington Accord standards. It is mandatory for engineers seeking skilled migration.

This report must also include Summary Statement form and Self Assessment form. For a more detailed format, check out the full KA02 format.

And, to avoid KA02 writing mistakes, you can contact us for KA02 report writing service.

3. CV

A detailed and well-structured CV with your work history is highly recommended by Engineering NZ. It provides assessors with an overview of your professional background. Your CV should not be more than three pages.

4. Application fee payment proof

Payment confirmation for the KA02 assessment fee.

5. Additional Supporting Document

  • Continuing Professional Development (CPD):

    It is not mandatory for the Knowledge Assessment application. However, it is recommended to keep a record of CPD activities. The record should include activity name, activity type, start and end date, CPD hours, and learning outcome.

  • Passport copy:

    A certified copy of your valid passport photo page.

  • Professional registrations:

    If you are already registered as a professional engineer in another country, provide evidence of your registration.

  • Language test results:

    If English is not your native language, you may need to submit an English language test result, like IELTS or TOEFL, meeting the required score.

Additional Note:

  • All documents must be submitted in English or accompanied by certified translations.
  • Check the latest version of the KA02 assessment guidelines for the most up-to-date information.

Now, after preparing everything and submitting your KA02 application, what should you expect? We recommend familiarising yourself with the following information below regarding the next phases of the process.

What happens in the Knowledge Assessment Process?

  • Outcome

After completing the application, the knowledge assessment process begins. Here is a step-by-step breakdown of the process:

  • Step 1: Application Review

    Once you submit your application, an assessor will be assigned to review your application.

  • Step 2: Interactive Assessment

    The interactive assessment is done through a video conference to discuss your application in detail. This usually lasts 60-90 minutes.

  • Step 3: Challenge Tests

    You might need to take some extra tests like oral and/or written examination, work simulation, and case study.

  • Step 4: Final Verdict

    After everything is reviewed, you will be notified of the final decision.

What Are the Possible Outcomes of the Knowledge Assessment?

Based on your assessment, there are 3 possible outcomes of the assessment:

  • Possible Outcome 1: Positive Assessment
  • Possible Outcome 2: Instruction To Bridge Knowledge Gaps
  • Possible Outcome 3: Negative Assessment

Possible Outcome 1: Positive Assessment

  • If you meet all the requirements and have shown competency level of a Washington Accord graduate, you will be awarded a positive assessment.
  • Now, you can request for a CPEng application through the online portal.

Possible Outcome 2: Instruction To Bridge Knowledge Gaps

  • If your skills do not adequately match Washington Accord standards, the assessor will pinpoint element(s) to improve on and suggest ways to bridge knowledge gaps.
    Remember:
  • If your report says to bridge knowledge gaps on either one or two elements, your further knowledge assessment will be free.
  • If you are asked to bridge knowledge gaps on 3 or more elements, then the knowledge assessment will be $500 (excluding GST) In both cases, you must provide evidence of activities undertaken within 12 months.

Possible Outcome 3: Negative Assessment

A detailed and well-structured CV with your work history is highly recommended by Engineering NZ. It provides assessors with an overview of your professional background. Your CV should not be more than three pages.
