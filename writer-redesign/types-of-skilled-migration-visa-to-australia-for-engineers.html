A Guide for Types of Skilled Migration Visa to Australia for International Engineers

Australia has developed various options of skilled migration visas focusing on international students and skilled workers. These visas are specifically tailored to provide opportunities for engineering students who have completed their studies and want to migrate permanently to Australia. The migration law of Australia is complicated and every migration provision varies from one another. This comprehensive guide contains all the necessary information about the available visa options including the types of skilled migration visa to Australia.

Overview of the Engineering Industry in Australia

Opportunities for International Engineers in Australia

Australia is undoubtedly one of the best destinations for individuals seeking exciting opportunities and rewarding careers. The government of Australia has implemented various programs for streamlined visa processes, occupation lists, and points-based systems to facilitate the entry of skilled migrants.

Here are some benefits for international students in Australia:

  • Multiple opportunities to work on different projects for a secure future.
  • The diverse engineering industry in Australia with potential benefits.
  • Access to a wide range of sectors to broaden skills and expertise.
  • Competitive salaries are offered in the engineering industry.
  • Excellent work-life balance.
  • High standard of living for engineering professionals in Australia.

Demand for international engineers in Australia

The demand for engineers in Australia is currently at its highest point in the past decade and is expected to continue growing. As a country known for providing abundant opportunities, Australia offers a range of job prospects with diverse scopes. Among the various occupations available, engineering is particularly appealing and is often favoured by international students. Recognizing the importance of this field, the Australian government is actively working to grant more Permanent Residency (PR) visas to qualified engineering applicants. The demand for engineers in Australia is not only significant for the development of the country but also a critical factor in staying competitive in the global business landscape. To meet this demand, Australia seeks skilled and professional engineers who can contribute to ongoing growth and innovation.

Engineers Australia (EA), the professional body accountable of representing engineers in Australia recognizes the following occupational categories:

  • Professional Engineer
  • Engineering Technologist
  • Engineering Associate
  • Engineering Manager

Types of Skilled Migration Visas for International Engineers

There are different types of skilled migration visa to Australia available for those engineering students who are interested in applying to Australia. These visa options are specially designed according to the needs of international students who are pursuing engineering jobs in Australia. Some of the skilled migration visa list are as follows:

1. Employer Nominated Scheme subclass 186 visa

The 186 direct entry employer-sponsored visa offers the chance to obtain permanent residency in Australia. This visa category operates on a points-based system, wherein eligibility is determined by scoring. To be eligible for the visa, applicants must meet the Subclass 186 Visa Requirements:

  • Possessing the required qualifications for the nominated occupation is essential.
  • A positive skills assessment is mandatory to demonstrate the applicant's expertise.
  • Age requirements must be met according to the visa guidelines.
  • Proficiency in the English language is necessary to ensure effective communication.
  • Meeting health requirements is a prerequisite for the visa application.
  • Demonstrating good character is important, as applicants need to meet character requirements.
  • The visa application allows for including dependent family members.
  • Certain documents must be provided as part of the application process.

2. Skilled (Independent) subclass 189 visa

The Skilled Independent 189 Visa allows individuals to live, work, and study in Australia on a permanent basis.

  • The Skilled Independent (Points-tested) stream requires applicants to express their interest in obtaining this visa category.
  • For New Zealand citizens, there is a specific stream called the Skilled Independent visa (subclass 189) (New Zealand) stream.
  • The visa application process involves a points-tested system, where applicants are assigned scores based on various factors.
  • Typically, a minimum score of 90 is required to receive an invitation to apply for the visa.
  • The Skilled Independent visa also known as the 189 visa Australia, provides the opportunity for permanent residency, offering individuals the freedom to reside, work, and study in Australia without the need for employer sponsorship or nomination by a state or territory government.

3. Skilled (State Sponsored) subclass 190 visa

The 190 visa is also a points-based visa category that enables skilled workers to live and work in Australia as permanent residents. To be eligible for this visa, applicants must meet the following 190 visa requirements:

  • Have a relevant occupation listed on the 190 visa occupation list.
  • Possess a suitable skills assessment for the nominated occupation.
  • Meet the minimum points test pass mark of 65 points (as of March 2022).
  • Be nominated by a state or territory government agency in Australia.
  • Lodge an Expression of Interest (EOI) and receive an invitation to apply for the visa.
  • Be aged between 18 and 44, inclusive.
  • Demonstrate competent English language skills.
  • Meet the health and character requirements specified by the Australian government.

4. Skilled—Recognised Graduate visa subclass 476

The Skilled Graduate visa (Subclass 476) is designed for recent engineering graduates. It allows them to live, work, and study in Australia for up to 18 months. To meet the Skilled Recognised Graduate Visa 476 visa requirements, applicants must have completed an engineering degree within the past 2 years and be under 31 years of age. This skilled migration visa Australia offers engineering graduates the opportunity to gain work experience, pursue further education, and travel within Australia while their visa is valid.

5. Temporary Skill Shortage Visa (Subclass 482)

The Temporary Skill Shortage Visa also known as the Subclass 482 visa Australia, has replaced the previous Subclass 457 Visa. It helps businesses with genuine skill shortages by sponsoring foreign workers, including engineers. The visa has three streams: Short-Term (up to two years), Medium-Term (up to four years), and Labour Agreement. Holders can live, work, study, include family, and potentially transition to permanent residency. Eligibility requires an Australian employer sponsor.

6. Temporary Graduate Visa (subclass 485)

The Temporary Graduate visa is a favourable option for engineers who have completed their studies at an Australian educational institution located in a regional area. The 485 visa offers temporary residence, work rights, and the opportunity to pursue further education in Australia. Available for holders of the Temporary Graduate visa under the Post-Study Work or Replacement stream, it allows them to gain practical work experience, explore cultural and professional opportunities, and potentially opens pathways to permanent residency for eligible applicants.

7. Skilled Work Regional (provisional) visa subclass 491

The Subclass 491 visa is a temporary visa that allows you to live, work, and study in designated regional areas of Australia as skilled worker along with your family. You must be nominated by an Australian state or territory government agency or sponsored by an eligible family member living in a designated regional area in order to apply for the 491 visa. This visa offers you the opportunity to live and work in your allocated regional area for a time period of up to 5 years.

Visa 491 is introduced to replace the previous subclass 489 visa(both family-sponsored and state/territory nominated). To be eligible for the 491 visa Australia, you must meet certain conditions, such as having a relevant occupation, a positive EA Skills Assessment, and meeting the required points, etc. Although this visa is temporary, it opens up the possibility for you to apply for permanent residency through the subclass 191 Permanent Residence (Skilled Regional) visa after you’ve lived in the regional area for 3 years and meet the Australian Department of Home Affairs' criteria.

8. Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (provisional) subclass 494 (employer sponsored stream)

The Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) Visa Subclass 494 allows engineers to reside, work, and study in Australia for a maximum of 5 years. Eligibility criteria include being nominated by an approved work sponsor, being under 45 years old, having an occupation on the skilled occupation list, and meeting language and skills assessment requirements. The 494 visa offers different streams, such as the Employer Sponsored, Labour Agreement, and Subsequent Entrant streams. It is important to accurately fulfil the visa requirements and submit the application to ensure timely processing.

9. Regional Sponsored Migration Scheme subclass 187 visa

The Regional Sponsored Migration Scheme Visa Subclass 187 is a work visa that permits you to reside and work in regional areas of Australia as a skilled worker with a nomination by a regional Australian employer. You need to fulfill certain eligibility requirements and prepare proper documents in order to apply for 187 visa. This visa provides you with permanent residence in Australia, granting you the freedom to live in the country indefinitely.

The Subclass 187 visa offers two streams: the Direct Entry Stream and the Temporary Residence Transition Stream. The Direct Entry Stream has been closed since 15th November 2019 and has been replaced by the Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) – Subclass 494 Visa. However, you can still apply for the visa 187 through the Temporary Residence Transition Stream. You will get several benefits with this visa such as being able to bring your family members along with you, getting enrolled in Medicare,etc.

10. Training Subclass 407 visa

The Training Subclass 407 Visa allows engineers to engage in occupational training or professional development in Australia. It has a maximum validity of 2 years and requires applicants to meet specific requirements, including being at least 18 years old, having functional English proficiency, possessing private health insurance, and intending to stay temporarily in Australia. Sponsorship by an approved Temporary Activities Sponsor is necessary. There are various types of eligible training, such as training for registration or licensing, overseas qualification, government-supported training, and professional development. Work limitations apply, and the visa application must be submitted online.

Choosing the Right Visa Pathway

Understanding the differences between the types of Skilled Migration Visa to Australia is essential especially for engineers, when selecting the right pathway for your immigration journey. Work visas are for individuals seeking employment abroad, while student visas are for those pursuing education. Tourist visas allow for leisure travel, and business visas facilitate business activities. Family visas enable joining immediate family members in a foreign country. Research and consultation with immigration authorities or professionals can help determine the most suitable visa type based on your purpose of travel, duration of stay, financial resources, and intended activities. Familiarity with these differences enhances your chances of a successful visa application for engineers looking to immigrate to Australia.

Factors to Consider When Selecting a Visa Pathway:

1. Purpose of Visit:

- Determine the main reason for your visit, such as work, study, tourism, business activities, or family reunification.

2. Eligibility Requirements:

- Understand the specific criteria for each visa type, including skills, qualifications, work experience, age, or sponsorship.

3. Visa Validity and Conditions:

- Consider the duration of the visa and any associated limitations or restrictions on work, study, or other activities.

4. Processing Time and Costs:

- Evaluate the expected processing time for visa applications and the associated fees to ensure they align with your timeline and budget.

5. Pathway to Permanent Residency:

- If your goal is to become a permanent resident, explore Australia's Skilled Migration with a PR Visa that offers a pathway to permanent residency.

6. Sponsorship Opportunities:

- If you have family members or employers willing to sponsor your visa application, investigate visa categories that allow for sponsorship.

7. Language and Cultural Factors:

- Be aware of any language requirements or cultural integration obligations associated with the visa pathway.

8. Health and Insurance:

- Understand any health requirements, such as medical examinations, and ensure you have adequate health insurance coverage.

9. Financial Considerations:

- Evaluate the financial implications of the visa pathway, including application fees, living expenses, tuition fees, and potential employment opportunities.

10. Professional Advice:

- Seek guidance from registered migration agents or immigration lawyers to receive expert assistance and tailored advice throughout the visa application process.
