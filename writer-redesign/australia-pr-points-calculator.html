Frequently Asked Question

A minimum of 65 points is required to apply for PR through the Skill Select program in Australia. However, it is important to note that simply scoring the minimum number of points does not guarantee that you will receive PR.

The number of points you are awarded depends on various factors, such as your age, work experience, English language proficiency, and qualifications. The exact number of points required for PR can also change over time as the Australian government adjusts its immigration policies.

PR points in Australia are used to determine an individual's eligibility for permanent residency. Points are calculated based on factors such as age, language proficiency, work experience, education, and other skills. Different visa programs have different point requirements, so it is important to be aware of the specific program being applied for. The general guideline for calculating PR points includes age (25-32 years old, max 25 points), English proficiency (20 points), work experience (based on type, duration, and skill level), education (max 20 points), a skilled spouse/partner, and other factors such as a job offer or points.

To get Australian Permanent Residency (PR), you need to meet the eligibility criteria and score enough points on the Points Test. The Points Test is a part of the process to apply for skilled migration visas, such as the Subclass 189 visa, the Subclass 190 visa, or the Subclass 491 visa.

Here are some factors that can help you score points on the points test:

  • Age: You can get the most points if you are under the age of 45.
  • English language proficiency: You can earn points for your proficiency in English based on the results of a recognized English language test.
  • Work experience: Points can be awarded for work experience in your nominated or closely related occupation.
  • Educational qualifications: You can get points for your educational qualifications, including a PhD or master's degree.
  • Skilled spouse or de facto partner: If your spouse or partner is skilled and has a suitable occupation, you may be able to get additional points.
  • State sponsorship: If you are sponsored by an Australian state or territory, you may be eligible for additional points.
  • Nomination by an Australian employer: If you have a genuine job offer from an Australian employer, you may be eligible for additional points.
