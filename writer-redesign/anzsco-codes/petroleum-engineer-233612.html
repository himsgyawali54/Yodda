Petroleum Engineer - ANZSCO 233612

Lets Chat
Get Free Consultation

Description

Plans and directs the engineering aspects of locating and extracting petroleum or natural gas from the earth. Focuses on exploring, producing, and utilising oil and gas resources. Petroleum Engineers design and develop oil and gas wells, including drilling, completion, and production operations. They also ensure that oil and gas production is efficient and safe while minimising environmental impact. Registration or licensing may be required.

Specialisation

  1. Mud Engineer
  2. Petrophysical Engineer

Average Income

In Australia, the average salary for a petroleum engineer varies depending on experience, qualifications, and job location. According to Payscale, as of March 2023, the average salary for a petroleum engineer in Australia is around AU$ 133,000 annually. However, this figure can range from around AU$76,000 to AU$247,000 per year, depending on the specific job and the company offering the position.

Unit Group 2336: Mining Engineers

Occupations in Unit Group 2336

- Mining Engineer 233611

- Petroleum Engineer 233612

The occupational category labelled as Unit Group 2336 under the Australian and New Zealand Standard Classification of Occupations (ANZSCO Codes) pertains to the profession of "Mining Engineers, Petroleum and Related Professionals." It encompasses individuals responsible for designing, developing, and supervising mining operations and extracting, processing, and purifying metals and minerals.

Source: Australian Bureau of Statistics and Department of Immigration and Border Protection

Indicative Skill Level

Skill Level: 1

ANZSCO Skill Level Classification 1 for a Petroleum Engineer is an entry-level position. This level of skill would involve working under close supervision and primarily performing routine and essential tasks in the field of petroleum engineering. Indicative Skill Level 1 for a Petroleum Engineer typically includes entry-level knowledge and skills in the field of petroleum engineering.

Skills Assessment Authority

For Australia

In Australia, the Skills Assessment Authority for Petroleum Engineers is Engineers Australia. Engineers Australia conducts a Competency Demonstration Report (CDR) assessment to determine whether an applicant's engineering qualifications and work experience are equivalent to Australian standards.

For New Zealand

In New Zealand, Engineering New Zealand assesses the skills, qualifications, and experience of Petroleum Engineers who wish to migrate to New Zealand under the Skilled Migrant Category.

Tasks to Perform for Petroleum Engineers

Here are some everyday tasks performed by Petroleum engineers:

  1. Conducting fundamental geological and reservoir analyses to determine the characteristics of oil and gas reservoirs.
  2. Assisting in designing and implementing drilling and completion programs for oil and gas wells.
  3. Monitoring and analysing healthy performance, including production rates and fluid properties.
  4. Assisting in developing plans to increase the productivity of existing wells.
  5. Performing essential economic evaluations of oil and gas projects to assess their profitability.
  6. Conducting safety and environmental assessments of oil and gas operations.
  7. Preparing technical reports and presentations on findings and recommendations.

Exceptions

  1. A higher degree in Petroleum Engineering or a related field such as Geology, Geophysics, or Reservoir Engineering may be required for senior-level positions.
  2. Employers may require years of experience working in the oil and gas industry to demonstrate a deep understanding of industry practices and standards.
  3. Professional certifications such as the Professional Engineer (PE) or Chartered Engineer (CEng) may be required for certain positions.
  4. Some employers may require specialised knowledge in a specific area, such as drilling, production, or reservoir engineering.
