Structural Engineer - ANZSCO 233214

Lets Chat
Get Free Consultation

Description

Analyses the statical properties of all types of structures, tests the behaviour and durability of materials used in their construction, and designs and supervises the construction of all types of structures. Structural Engineers use their knowledge of physics, mechanics, and materials science to ensure that systems are safe, functional, and cost-effective. They also consider environmental factors such as earthquakes and wind loads. Registration or licensing may be required.

Average Income

The average income for a structural engineer in Australia varies depending on experience, location, and industry. According to data from PayScale, as of March 2023, the average salary for a structural engineer in Australia is around AUD 86,000 per year. However, this can range from around AUD 60,000 for entry-level positions to over AUD 140,000 for those with more experience or working in specialised fields.

Unit Group 2332: Civil Engineering Professionals

Occupations in Unit Group 2332

- Civil Engineer 233211

- Geotechnical Engineer 233212

- Structural Engineer 233214

- Transport Engineer 233215

Unit Group 2332 in Engineering Australia refers to the "Civil Engineering Professionals occupation group." This unit group includes occupations primarily involved in designing, constructing, and maintaining the physical and natural built environment, including buildings, infrastructure, and other structures. This group are categorized by their specific ANZSCO Codes and encompasses several areas of expertise, including Civil engineering (233211), Geotechnical engineering (233212), Structural engineering (233214), and Transport Engineering (233215).

Source: Australian Bureau of Statistics and Department of Immigration and Border Protection

Indicative Skill Level

Skill Level: 1

ANZSCO Skill Level 1 for Structural Engineers is the highest level of skill proficiency as defined by the Australian and New Zealand Standard Classification of Occupations (ANZSCO). This level refers to individuals with a high level of knowledge and experience in the field who can carry out complex tasks and responsibilities independently. They are typically required to have a Bachelor's degree or higher in civil or structural engineering and several years of relevant work experience. At this level, Structural Engineers are expected to possess a wide range of technical and professional skills and strong communication and interpersonal skills to work effectively with clients and other professionals in the industry.

Skills Assessment Authority

For Australia

In Australia, the Skills Assessment Authority for Structural Engineers is Engineers Australia. Engineers Australia conducts a Competency Demonstration Report (CDR) assessment to determine whether an applicant's engineering qualifications and work experience are equivalent to Australian standards.

For New Zealand

In New Zealand, Engineering New Zealand assesses the skills, qualifications, and experience of Structural Engineers who wish to migrate to New Zealand under the Skilled Migrant Category.

Tasks to Perform for Structural Engineer

  1. Design and analyse structures such as buildings, bridges, and tunnels.
  2. Ensure systems are safe, functional, and meet relevant codes and standards.
  3. Develop and review technical specifications and drawings.
  4. Perform calculations and simulations to test and validate designs.
  5. Work with other professionals such as architects, builders, and project managers.
  6. Manage projects and budgets, and prepare progress reports.
  7. Monitor and inspect construction sites to ensure compliance with designs and specifications.
  8. Investigate and diagnose structural problems, and develop solutions.
  9. Use computer-aided design (CAD) software and other tools to assist with design and analysis.

Exceptions

There are some exceptions to the typical requirements for geotechnical engineers who wish to work in Australia. Some exceptions include the following:

  1. Some visas may waive the English language requirement for applicants from English-speaking countries, such as the United Kingdom, Canada, and the United States.
  2. Some visas may have lower English language requirements for applicants sponsored by an Australian employer or who have completed tertiary education in Australia.
  3. Some visas may not require an English language test if the applicant can demonstrate that they have worked in a skilled occupation in Australia for at least five years.
  4. Some visas may waive the English language requirement if the applicant has a medical condition that makes taking an English language test difficult.
