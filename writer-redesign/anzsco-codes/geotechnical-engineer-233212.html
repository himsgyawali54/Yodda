Geotechnical Engineer - ANZSCO 233212

Lets Chat
Get Free Consultation

Description

Plans, directs and conducts survey work to analyse the likely behaviour of soil and rock when placed under pressure by proposed structures, and designs above and below ground foundations. Geotechnical Engineers use this knowledge to design and analyse structures such as foundations, retaining walls, and tunnels. Registration or licensing may be required.

Average Income

The average salary for a geotechnical engineer in Australia is around AUD 90,000 annually. However, this can range from approximately AUD 60,000 annually for an entry-level geotechnical engineer to over AUD 150,000 for a highly experienced professional. Salaries can also vary by location, with major cities like Sydney and Melbourne offering higher wages than regional areas.

Unit Group 2332: Civil Engineering Professionals

Occupations in Unit Group 2332

- Civil Engineer 233211

- Geotechnical Engineer 233212

- Structural Engineer 233214

- Transport Engineer 233215

In the Australian and New Zealand Standard Classification of Occupations (ANZSCO Codes), "Civil Engineering Professionals" is the category to which Unit Group 2332 is assigned. This group encompasses several areas of expertise, including Civil engineering (233211), Geotechnical engineering (233212), Structural engineering (233214), and Transport Engineering (233215). Typically, a Bachelor's degree or higher education is necessary to pursue a career in this field, and Civil Engineering Professionals are highly sought after in Australia.

Source: Australian Bureau of Statistics and Department of Immigration and Border Protection

Indicative Skill Level

Skill Level: 1

In the context of Australian migration, a Geotechnical Engineer at Indicative Skill Level 1 refers to a highly skilled professional capable of independently performing complex tasks. According to the ANZSCO Skill Level Classification, Geotechnical Engineers at this level possess a Bachelor's degree or higher qualification and have several years of relevant work experience. They are proficient in designing, planning, and supervising the construction of infrastructure projects such as tunnels, dams, and bridges. Geotechnical Engineers at this skill level may also lead a team of professionals and can make strategic decisions in their expertise.

Skills Assessment Authority

For Australia

In Australia, the Skills Assessment Authority for Geotechnical Engineers is Engineers Australia. Engineers Australia conducts a Competency Demonstration Report (CDR) assessment to determine whether an applicant's engineering qualifications and work experience are equivalent to Australian standards.

For New Zealand

In New Zealand, Engineering New Zealand assesses the skills, qualifications, and experience of Geotechnical Engineers who wish to migrate to New Zealand under the Skilled Migrant Category.

Tasks to Perform for Geotechnical Engineer

  1. Geotechnical Engineers are responsible for investigating earth materials' properties and designing engineering works that interact with soil, rock, and groundwater.
  2. They may work on tunnels, bridges, buildings, mining, and oil and gas exploration projects. Their duties include:
    • Conducting soil and rock testing.
    • Analysing and interpreting data.
    • Designing geotechnical structures.
    • Monitoring construction sites to ensure compliance with design specifications.
  3. They usually work for engineering consulting firms, government agencies, or construction companies.

Exceptions

There are some exceptions to the typical requirements for geotechnical engineers who wish to work in Australia. Some exceptions include the following:

  1. Engineers who have completed a recognized Ph.D. in Geotechnical Engineering may be eligible for exemptions from the usual work experience requirements.
  2. Engineers who have completed a recognized Masters's degree in Geotechnical Engineering may be eligible for credit toward the work experience requirements for professional registration.
  3. Engineers who have completed a recognized undergraduate degree in a related field (e.g., Civil Engineering) and have substantial work experience in geotechnical engineering may be eligible for professional registration.
  4. Engineers who have completed their engineering education in a country with an engineering accreditation agreement with Australia may be eligible for accreditation as professional engineers in Australia.
