Chemical Engineer- ANZSCO 233111

Lets Chat
Get Free Consultation

Description

Chemical Engineer designs and prepares specifications for chemical process systems and the construction and operation of commercial-scale chemical plants, and supervises industrial processing and fabrication of products undergoing physical and chemical changes. Registration or licensing may be required.

Graduates in chemical engineering can find employment in multiple industries, including mining, energy, pharmaceuticals, food and beverage, and water treatment. The Australian government also offers various funding opportunities and research grants for chemical engineering research and development, particularly in sustainable energy and the environment.

Average Income

The average salary for a chemical engineer in Australia is around AUD 98,000 annually. However, this can vary depending on experience level, industry sector, and location. For example, a chemical engineer with more experience or working in the mining industry may earn a higher salary than someone just starting in the field or working in a different sector.

Unit Group 2331: Chemical and Materials Engineer

Occupations in Unit Group 2331

- Chemical Engineer 233111

- Materials Engineer 233112

Unit Group 2331 refers to "Chemical and Materials Engineers" in the Australian and New Zealand Standard Classification of Occupations (ANZSCO). This unit group includes professionals researching, designing, and developing chemical processes and materials for various products and applications.

Chemical and Materials Engineers work in various industries, including manufacturing, pharmaceuticals, energy production, and environmental management. They may develop new products, optimise manufacturing processes, or improve existing materials and products.

Source: Australian Bureau of Statistics and Department of Immigration and Border Protection

Indicative Skill Level

Skill Level: 1

The ANZSCO Codes provides a system for classifying occupations regarding skill level, with Skill Level 1 being the highest level of skill required. The indicative skill level for Chemical Engineers in Australia is Skill Level 1. To work as a Chemical Engineer in Australia, individuals are generally required to have a bachelor's degree or higher qualification in Chemical Engineering, often requiring significant work experience in the field.

ANZSCO Skill Level 1 jobs are typically considered highly skilled and require a high level of knowledge, expertise, and experience in a specific field or industry. They are usually managerial or professional roles that involve complex decision-making and require high autonomy and responsibility.

Skills Assessment Authority

For Australia

In Australia, the Skills Assessment Authority for Chemical Engineer is Engineers Australia. Engineers Australia conducts a Competency Demonstration Report (CDR) assessment to determine whether an applicant's engineering qualifications and work experience are equivalent to Australian standards.

For New Zealand

In New Zealand, Engineering New Zealand assesses the skills, qualifications, and experience of Chemical Engineer who wish to migrate to New Zealand under the Skilled Migrant Category. They conduct assessments to ensure that applicants meet the necessary standards for registration as a Chartered Professional Engineer.

Tasks to Perform for Chemical Engineer

The tasks a Chemical Engineer performs may include

  1. Planning, directing, and coordinating chemical manufacturing processes.
  2. Managing and leading a team of chemical engineers and technicians.
  3. Developing and implementing quality control measures to ensure product consistency.
  4. Conducting research and development activities to improve processes and products.
  5. Chemical Engineers work in various industries, such as manufacturing, pharmaceuticals, and mining.
  6. They are involved in developing new products, improving existing ones, and ensuring that manufacturing processes are efficient and safe.
  7. They also play a crucial role in addressing environmental issues, such as reducing waste and emissions.

Exceptions

There are some exceptions to the English language requirements for Chemical Engineers to migrate to Australia. Here are three possible exceptions:

  1. If you are a citizen of the United Kingdom, Canada, New Zealand, the United States of America, or the Republic of Ireland. In that case, you may be exempt from the English language requirements.
  2. If you have completed at least five years of full-time study in a secondary and tertiary education institution where the medium of instruction was in English. In that case, you may be exempt from the English language requirements.
  3. Suppose you have worked full-time in a professional occupation for at least three years where the primary language of communication was English. In that case, you may be exempt from the English language requirements. This option may be available for specific visa programs, such as the Skilled Independent visa (subclass 189).
