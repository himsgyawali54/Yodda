Electronics Engineer - ANZSCO 233411

Lets Chat
Get Free Consultation

Description

Design, develop, adapt, install, test and maintain electronic components, circuits and systems used for computer systems, communication systems, entertainment, transport and other industrial applications. They also analyse and solve problems related to electronic systems and ensure they are safe and reliable.

Specialisation

  1. Communications Engineer (Army)

Average Income

The average salary for an electronics engineer in Australia varies depending on experience, location, and the specific industry in which they work. According to the Australian Government Department of Jobs and Small Business, the median salary for electronics engineers in Australia is around AUD 95,000 annually. However, this can range from around AUD 70,000 to AUD 135,000 or more, depending on various factors. It's worth noting that salaries may also vary based on qualifications, skills, and specialisation in electronics engineering.

Unit Group 2334: Electronics Engineers

Occupations in Unit Group 2334

- Electronics Engineer 233411

According to the ANZSCO Codes, the Unit Group 233411 refers to Electronics Engineers, a profession that involves designing, developing, and maintaining electronic systems and components. Electronics Engineers typically work on various electronic devices and techniques, such as communication, control, power generation, and medical equipment.

Source: Australian Bureau of Statistics and Department of Immigration and Border Protection

Indicative Skill Level

Skill Level: 1

For Electronics Engineers, ANZSCO Skill Level Classification 1 implies that the engineer is expected to have a high knowledge of electronic engineering principles and demonstrate expertise in designing, developing, and testing electronic systems and components.

They must understand computer hardware and software, electrical circuits,microprocessors, and other electronic components. Additionally, they must be able to use specialised computer-aided design (CAD) software to create and analyse electronic formats.

Skills Assessment Authority

For Australia

In Australia, the Skills Assessment Authority for Electronics Engineers is Engineers Australia. Engineers Australia conducts an assessment of CDR Report for Electronics Engineers to determine whether an applicant's engineering qualifications and work experience are equivalent to Australian standards.

For New Zealand

Tasks to Perform for Electronics Engineers

Here are some everyday tasks performed by electronic engineers:

  1. Designing and developing electronic systems and devices, such as circuit boards, microcontrollers, and sensors.
  2. Researching to develop new technologies or improve existing ones.
  3. Creating and testing prototypes of electronic devices to ensure they function as intended.
  4. Collaborating with other engineers, technicians, and project managers to develop project plans and ensure projects are completed on time and within budget.
  5. Troubleshooting and resolving issues with electronic systems and devices.
  6. Developing software and firmware for electronic devices and systems.
  7. Ensuring compliance with regulatory standards and safety requirements.
  8. Conducting performance testing and analysis of electronic systems and components.
  9. Creating technical documentation and reports.
  10. Providing technical support and training to customers and colleagues.

Exceptions

  1. Electronics engineers may require a licence to practise certain specialised services in Australia.
  2. If you are a non-Australian citizen, you may require a valid visa to work as an electronics engineer in Australia.
  3. Electronics engineers who wish to work on safety-critical systems such as medical devices may require certification by the relevant regulatory authority in Australia.
  4. Electronics engineers in specific aviation, telecommunications, or defence industries may be subject to additional rules and requirements.
