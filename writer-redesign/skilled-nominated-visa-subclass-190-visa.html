State Sponsored Skilled Nominated Visa Subclass 190 Visa

Every year thousands of ambitious individuals migrate to Australia in search of better job prospects and career opportunities. These skilled workers come from various corners of the globe, driven by the allure of Australia's thriving economy, diverse workforce, and enviable quality of life. Among the different visa options available, the State-sponsored skilled nominated visa, also known as subclass 190 visa, holds a special place. The Visa 190 Australia program facilitates personal growth and professional development and catalyses the economic success of Australia.

What is a Subclass 190 visa?

A subclass 190 visa is a permanent work permit that allows overseas skilled professionals to work and live in Australia. It is one of the pathways to Skilled Migration in Australia. You require a territory government or an Australian state nomination to apply for a subclass 190 Skilled Nominated visa. Besides, applicants must meet certain eligibility criteria, including possessing specific skills and obtaining a minimum of 65 assessment points, for Australia permanent residency through Visa 190.

Permanent residents in Australia are permitted to stay and work anywhere in Australia, study, and sponsor family members. The applicants for the state-sponsored skilled nominated visa can include family members in their application.

Who Can Apply For A Visa 190 Australia?

Applicants seeking to live in a specified state or territory of Australia must meet certain criteria to apply for a Visa 190 Australia. Below are listed the eligibility criteria to apply for a 190 visa.

  • An Australian state must nominate you for skilled migration to the country.
  • Your age is between 18 and 45 inclusive, as points are assigned according to the age groups.
  • You have had your Registration of Interest (ROI) selected.
  • Your occupation is on a relevant occupation list.
  • You meet the required points for the subclass 190 skilled nominated visa you intend to lodge.

Skilled Nominated Subclass 190 Visa Checklist

The table below showcases the documents you need for a 190 visa application.

Identity Documents You must include identity documents such as your passport, birth certificate, personal details, national identity card, and proof of name change such as a marriage or divorce certificate.
Character Documents You have to provide an overseas police certificate from your homeland as well as the country you have visited, where you spent a total of 12 months or more in the last 10 years since you turned 16. You can also provide any military service records if you have served in a country's armed forces.
EOI (Expression of Interest documents) You have to be able to provide all the documents that you have claimed in your EOI.
Skilled Assessment Documents You must submit all the academic certificates and transcripts, course completion letter, professional year certificate (if you have one), NAATI certificate (if you have one), Evidence of any specialist qualification, skill assessment outcome letter, any professional registration or licence certificate, your curriculum vitae or resume.
Competent English proficiency documents English proficiency test score. IELTS, PTE Academic, TOEFL or OET.
Relationship Documents You will need documents or statutory documents such as marriage certificates, divorce documents, or death certificates if you have been married, widowed, divorced, or permanently separated.
Partner Documents You need to provide necessary identity documents, character documents, and evidence of your relationship status, including marriage certificates (if applicable) or proof of a genuine de facto relationship for at least 12 months.
Documents for partner’s functional English If your dependent holds a valid passport from the UK, Ireland, USA, Canada, or New Zealand, you don't need to provide any English proficiency documents, but otherwise, you must show proof of functional English or pay an additional visa fee.
Dependents with over 18 documents To include your child (over 18) in your visa application, they must be dependent on you or your partner and meet certain age and dependency requirements. Proof of their dependency, identity documents, and evidence of financial support may be necessary. If they have disabilities and are turning 23, a medical report stating their dependency is required.
Scan or Photograph You should create digital copies of all your documents, both in English and non-English, by scanning or taking clear colour photographs. Ensure that each document is saved as a single file, even if it has multiple pages.

Benefits of Visa 190 Australia

Australia has been a popular destination for individuals seeking new opportunities and a high standard of living. The country is considered the land of opportunities among thousands of people willing to migrate and work in Australia as a permanent resident. Its immigration policy focuses on attracting skilled workers from all around the world who can contribute to the country's economy and fill in the market gaps. The skilled workers with a skilled nominated visa are provided with various privileges. Below are listed the benefits of skilled visa 190 in Australia.

1. Permanent Residency

Permanent residency is a type of immigration status where you get to live in Australia without any time limit. With an Australia PR, you no longer have to worry about the uncertainty of visas and the constant need to prove your eligibility to stay. You will become a part of Australian society, expanding connections and friendships that enrich your life. PR is also a pathway to Australian citizenship. After working for four years, you get to apply for citizenship of the country.

2. Access To Education And Training

Education is one of the most important factors for personal development and growth. When you have a 190 visa, you can access all the educational benefits provided by the Australian government to its citizens. You can access all the educational opportunities and training that Australian Institutions provide. Australia is focused on a quality education system and has various schooling systems.

3. Unrestricted Work Rights

After getting a state-sponsored visa subclass 190, you can work in any field you choose. With unrestricted work rights, you have the power to shape your own career path. You can pursue your passions and explore diverse industries, finding a job that aligns with your skills and interests. Whether you dream of working in a creative field, making a difference in the healthcare sector, or starting your own business, the possibilities are boundless.

4. Travel Rights

You can also travel to and from Australia for 5 years without restrictions. Travel rights enable you to visit family and friends, celebrate special occasions, and create lasting memories. Whether attending a family wedding, a reunion, or simply spending quality time with loved ones, these travel rights bridge the distance and bring people closer.

5. Social Benefits

You are welcomed into a new society with various social benefits the Australian government has to offer. These benefits create a better society where everyone has a fair chance to succeed and be happy. It includes access to essential services, support for those in need, measures to reduce inequality and poverty, healthcare provisions, educational opportunities, promotion of inclusivity, and community empowerment.

6. Family Sponsorship

You can also sponsor your eligible family members to join you in Australia. Family sponsorship is the process of sponsoring or supporting family members for Australia migration, with the intention of being together again. Family members who are already permanent residents or citizens of a country can sponsor their eligible relatives to join them in Austr.

7. Access To Medicare

You are eligible to access all national healthcare schemes in Australia. Under Medicare, eligible individuals can receive medical treatment, consultations with doctors, access to specialists, diagnostic tests, and prescription medications at reduced costs. The system aims to promote the well-being and health of Australian permanent residents and citizens, providing peace of mind and necessary support when it comes to healthcare needs.

8. Voting Rights

After you get permanent residency in Australia, you still don’t get voting rights. However, one with Australian PR can apply for the country’s citizenship which allows them to vote in elections. This means that you can actively contribute to the democratic process of the country.

Application Process for Visa 190 Australia

The application process requires submitting necessary documents, forms, and information to relevant authorities. It involves meeting eligibility criteria, providing supporting documentation, and undergoing background checks. The purpose is to assess an individual's qualifications for the desired program. Follow the steps below for a successful Visa 190 Australia application.

Step 1: Submit Your Skills Assessment Application

A Skills Assessment application is a procedure that examines one’s qualifications in a specific profession or field. You should first ensure that your occupation is on the skilled occupation list, aka ANZSCO code list.

Step 2: Complete and Submit An Expression of Interest (EOI)

An Expression of Interest (EOI) is a way to express interest in a specific visa category. It is a preliminary application that includes your personal details, work experience, qualifications, etc. While applying for Visa 190 Australia, you have to submit EOI that includes all the information required. Once you have submitted your EOI, the relevant authorities will review your application and then assess your eligibility for the visa.

Step 3: Submit Your Application For State Nomination

Once you’ve completed the necessary steps and got everything ready for your state nomination application, it’s time to gather all the required documents mentioned in the provided checklist. You have to ensure you’ve included all the important paperwork, like your identification documents, proof of qualifications, work history, and any other supporting materials they’ve asked for.

Step 4: Await Your Invitation To Apply For The Skilled Visa

Once your application has been reviewed and your eligibility assessed by the authorities, you will receive a mail if you have been selected to proceed. This invitation to apply is important, as it allows you to submit your complete application and provide any additional supporting documents that may be required. It means that you have met the initial criteria and have the opportunity to support your application further. To sum up, You now need to wait for an invitation to apply.

Step 5: Apply For State-sponsored 190 Skilled Visa

Once you get an invitation from a desired state, you can apply for a state-sponsored skilled nominated visa (subclass 190). Submit your visa application within 60 days of your invitation or else an additional charge may be added and there might be chances of rejection.

What is The Processing Time For A 190 Skilled Nominated Visa?

The processing time for application of 190 Skilled Nominated Visa can be completed within the timeframe of 10 months for 75% of applications, and possibly within 12 months for 90% of applications. As there is no standard processing time for visa 190 Australia, each visa is evaluated separately. The 190 visa processing time depends on the factors such as application volume, application completeness, and the demand for skilled workers.

190 Visa Requirements

The Skilled Nominated Subclass 190 visa is a permanent visa option for skilled workers, where eligibility is determined based on a points system where you have to fulfil all the requirements. The 190 visa point calculation determines your eligibility for the State-sponsored Skilled Nomination Visa (subclass 190) in Australia. Points are assessed based on factors such as age, English language proficiency, education qualifications, and work experience.

Registration of Interest (ROI)

Registration of interest is the initial step in which you express your interest in applying for a 190 visa and provide information about your experience, skills, qualifications, and other relevant factors. However, no points are assigned for ROI.

Skill Assessment Requirement

One of the key requirements for the 190 Visa (StateSponsored Skilled Nominated Visa) is that applicants must have their skills assessed by a relevant assessing authority.

For instance, as part of the visa application process, engineers are typically required to undergo a skill assessment by a recognized assessing authority in Australia. The assessing authority will evaluate the applicant's qualifications, work experience, and skills to ensure they meet the required standards for their nominated occupation. For engineering occupations, the assessing authority is typically Engineers Australia. The purpose of the skill assessment is to determine if the applicant's qualifications and experience align with the Australian standards and to verify their suitability for skilled migration to Australia through Visa 190.

Once the skill assessment is successfully completed, applicants can proceed with the visa application process. Meeting the skill assessment requirement is a crucial step for engineers to migrate to Australia on a subclass 190 visa.

How Much Does A 190 Visa Cost?

After you have provided all the required documents and lodged your Australia 190 visa, you need to pay the application fees. All primary applicants and dependent family members should pay for the application charge.

  • The cost of the Visa 190 Australia for the primary applicant is AUD$ 4240.
  • The Skilled Nominated Subclass 190 Visa fee cost for additional applicants, who is at least 18 years old, is AUD$ 2120.
  • The 190 visa fee for applicants below 18 years old is AUD$ 1060.

Note: You must submit your State-Sponsored Nominated Scheme visa application within 6 months of your nomination. As there may be a second instalment payable for some applicants before a certain decision is made on the application, there might be chances of your 190 visa rejection. of the application. However, if you submit your nomination and visa applications together, this time limit does not apply.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

You need a minimum of 65 points to apply for the 190 visa. These points are obtained on the basis of various factors such as age, English language proficiency, educational qualifications, and skill employment experience.

You will get nominated for a 190 visa in Australia if you submit all your documents along with an Expression of interest and receive a nomination from the state or territory government.

The processing time for a 190 nomination in New South Wales (NSW) is typically around six weeks. This is an approximate timeframe and can vary based on various factors. The NSW government is dedicated to efficiently processing nominations, but it's important to remember that they receive numerous applications, which may contribute to the processing time.

You can bring your family members with you on the Skilled Nominated Subclass 190 visa. This includes your spouse or partner, dependent children, and other dependent family members. If your visa application is approved, your family members will be granted the same visa subclass and can join you in Australia. They will have the opportunity to live, work, and study in Australia for the duration of the visa. Including all eligible family members in your application is important to ensure they are included in the visa approval.

The total visa fee for a couple applying for the Skilled Nominated Subclass 190 visa would be AUD $6360. This includes a base application fee of A$4240 for the primary applicant and an additional application fee of A$2120 for each applicant who is at least 18 years old.
