Skilled Occupation List Australia 2023

The Australian government has created policies to attract experienced migrants with the required skills through its skilled migration program. A goal of this is to bring people who can support the economy and staff jobs where local Australians are unavailable. If you are looking to live and work in Australia permanently, it might help to know the most desired jobs in Australia. To increase your chances at a permanent residency, consider taking a course that will lead to a rewarding career in an area that is highly desired in Australia.

Skilled Occupation List (SOL) Australia is a list of occupations eligible for an Australian immigration visa. It helps the government to identify potential skilled migrants who can fill labor shortages in the country. The list includes professions such as medical and engineering professionals, IT specialists, scientists, accountants and financial advisors, agriculturalists, teachers, and many more. The skilled occupation list in Australia is regularly updated and modified by the Department of Home Affairs to keep up with changing economic and labor needs.

Skilled Occupation List

The Skilled Occupation List Australia 2023 lists the current occupations that are qualified for various visa classifications. Visas are available to those competent to work or train in appropriately skilled employment in Australia and meet all other requirements.

  • Employer Nomination Scheme (subclass 186) – 186 (DE)
  • Skilled Independent Visa (subclass 189) – 189 (PT)
  • Skilled Nominated Visa (subclass 190) – 190
  • Training Visa (subclass 407) – 407
  • Temporary Work Visa (subclass 457) – 457
  • Temporary Graduate Visa (subclass 485) – 485 (GW)
  • Skilled Work Regional Visa Family Nominated (subclass 491) – 491 (F)
  • Skilled Work Regional Visa State or Territory Nominated (subclass 491) – 491 (S/T)
  • Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) Visa (subclass 494) – 494
  • Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) – Short-term stream – TSS (S)

Skilled Occupation Visa Type:

1. Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (provisional) visa (subclass 494):

This visa type allows regional employers to address identified skill/labor shortages in their region by sponsoring skilled workers where employers can’t source a suitably skilled Australian worker.

2. Employer Nomination Scheme (ENS) visa (subclass 186):

Allows skilled workers nominated by an employer to live and work in Australia permanently.

3. Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494):

Allows skilled workers to live, work, and study in regional Australia for five years if nominated by a regional employer.

4. Skilled Independent visa (subclass 189):

Skilled Independent 189 Visa allows skilled workers nominated by their employer to live and work in Australia permanently.

5. Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190):

Allows skilled workers to live and work in Australia as permanent residents.

6. Temporary Skill Shortage (TSS) visa (subclass 482):

Allows an employer to sponsor a skilled worker for a position they can’t fill with an Australian.

7. Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485):

Allows international students who have graduated with applicable qualifications to find occupations in demand in Australia.

8. Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491):

Allows skilled individuals nominated by a state or territory government to live and work in regional Australia.

9. Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (provisional) visa (subclass 494):

Allows regional employers to sponsor skilled workers where they can’t find suitably skilled Australian workers.

Types of Occupation Lists

Australia has three types of occupation lists: Medium and Long‑term Strategic Skills List (MLTSSL), Short‑term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL), and Regional Occupation List (ROL).

  • The MLTSSL list is meant for those applying for a Skilled Independent Subclass 189 non-sponsored skilled permanent visa, which enables the applicant to live and work in Australia permanently.
  • The STSOL list contains occupations in high demand in various states and territories. It is valid for applicants applying for a Skilled Nominated Subclass 190 Visa or Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) Visa Subclass 491.
  • The ROL is available for visa categories such as Subclass 407, Subclass 491, Subclass 494, and TSS (M), which are related to the regional migration program.

What’s the main purpose of Skilled Occupation?

The Skilled Occupation List (SOL) Australia is a list of occupations currently in demand in the Australian labor market. It is used by the Department of Home Affairs to determine which visas and occupations are eligible for skilled migration to Australia.

The list is updated regularly, depending on the labor market needs, and is an important tool for both employers and migrants to ascertain their eligibility for visa applications. The purpose of this list is to ensure that the skill level of migrants to Australia is matched to the skill needs of the Australian labor market, while also encouraging talented people to come and contribute to the Australian economy via skilled visas. It is used to identify what skills and qualifications are in demand in various industries and professions. This helps to give employers and migrants a clearer idea of the specific skills needed for successful visa applications. It can be used as a reference by employers to efficiently fill vacancies with individuals who have the appropriate skill levels and qualifications for the job. The SOL can also be used for other immigration purposes, such as determining which visas are eligible for applications. As such, the Skilled Occupation List Australia is a valuable resource for both employers and migrants alike.
