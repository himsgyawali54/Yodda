How to skill migrate to Australia as a Civil engineer?

Migrate To Australia As A Civil Engineer

The Australian Government regards Civil Engineer as one of the occupation required to develop the Australian economy. As a result, the processing of Civil Engineer nominations and skilled visa applications would take precedence. If you are a Civil Engineer looking to move to Australia with skilled migration and advance your career, this is the chance.

Brief Summary of Civil Engineering

Civil Engineering is one of Australia's most prestigious engineering disciplines. The design, maintenance, and building of new roadways, water filtration, water supply management, and urban real estate development are essential topics regarding skilled employment for Civil Engineers.

Due to the continuously growing population in Australia, infrastructure projects are overgrowing creating a temporary skill shortage. Due to this, there is a high demand for civil engineering professionals in this field who can competently fulfil the role required in this skilled occupation. The number of people working as civil engineers had reached 56,100 by 2019 and more than 80% of people were working full-time. Moving to Australia as a Civil Engineer is highly sought after due to these reasons.

The requirement to work in this field is mentioned below:

  • Should complete Bachelor of civil engineering
  • Should have Professional Engineerâ€™s Licence
  • Should have a full-time job in this occupation for at least 2 years

Salary: A Civil engineer' salary can range from 90,000 AUD to 108,000 AUD per year.

Tasks as a Civil Engineer

  • specifying construction techniques, materials methods, and procedures
  • managing site labour
  • delivery of construction materials
  • testing samples
  • having an understanding of architectural and engineering drawings
  • designing structures
  • developing the physical elements of transportation systems

Visa Options for Civil Engineers to Migrate Australia

Civil engineers can apply for a variety of visas to work or live in Australia, including:

  1. 482 Temporary Skill Shortage
  2. 186 Employer Nomination Scheme Visa
  3. 494 Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional Visa
  4. 407 Training Visa
  5. 485 Temporary Graduate
  6. 189 Skilled Independent Visa
  7. 190 Skilled Nominated Visa
  8. 491 Skilled Regional Visa (Provisional)

Civil Engineer Skill Assessment (ANZSCO Code: 233211)

The majority of jobs in Civil Engineering require a minimum level of skill equivalent to a bachelor's degree or higher. In addition to formal education, relevant experience and/or on-the-job training is also required (ANZSCO Skill Level 1) as stated by Engineers Australia.

There might be a need for licensing or registration.

Assessment Pathways For Australian Skills Migration

Accredited Qualifications

  1. Australian Qualification Pathway
  2. Washington Accord Pathway
  3. Sydney Accord Pathway
  4. Dublin Accord Pathway

Non-Accredited Qualifications

You might be able to apply for a skills assessment through the Competency Demonstration Report (CDR) Pathway if your qualification is not accredited.
The particular assessment path is determined by the country where you received your engineering qualification.

Required documents for skills assessments:

  • Passport-size photograph
  • Proof of Registration
  • CV
  • Name change documents
  • Education Qualifications

Difference Between 189 and 190 Visa : Skilled Independent & Nominated Visa

This is one of the most often asked questions among those considering relocating to Australia. The key distinction is that a Skilled Independent 189 Visa does not require sponsorship, whereas a Skilled Nominated Visa (190) requires that the applicant be "sponsored" by a state or territory.

If you have visa number 189, you can stay and work in Australia indefinitely. It is a long-term visa. Skilled Visa 189 must be applied for by competent and skillful workers who want to pursue a career in Australia. It costs about $4,115 AUD. Your eligible relatives may be sponsored to apply for permanent residency in Australia.

Those who are granted a "sponsored" visa have particular responsibilities about where they live that those who are granted an "independent" visa do not.

The method and regularity of the 189 and 190 visas also vary. The Federal Government typically sends out invitations for the 189 visa once a month for each occupation on the MLTSSL. Invitation rounds for the 189 visa are anticipated to run every three months, with the most recent round taking place in January 2021 and the following round scheduled for April 2021. This is due to the current economic climate brought on by COVID-19. These are separate from the invitation rounds for the states and territories.

The following are the requirements for a Skilled Nominated Visa:

  • Obtaining a nomination from a state or territory in Australia. This entails submitting a separate application to the state/territory and complying with their specific procedures.
  • After receiving clearance, you must relocate to your chosen state or territory within 6 months of arriving in Australia.
  • For at least two years, you must live and work in the state or territory you have chosen.

The following are the other two distinctions between the Skilled Independent Visa (189) and the Skilled Nominated Visa (190):

  • The Combined List of Eligible Skilled Occupations must be used to nominate occupations for a 190 visa. The vocation for a 189 visa, on the other hand, must be selected from the Medium and Long Term Strategic Skills List ('MLTSSL').
  • If you have a 190 visa, you can get an extra 5 points towards your points test elements if you don't reach the requirement.
