Top 10 Engineering Jobs in Australia 2023

The Land of Opportunities- Australia, we might have heard this word hundreds of times. In this rapidly evolving world where an engineering profession is becoming a more pivotal field that drives innovation and progress, Australia is a perfect country that offers the best career options for you. No matter if you are a recent graduate or a seasoned professional, there are top engineering jobs in Australia that fit you right, offering an amazing salary package and high standard of living.

This article will cover all the aspects of Engineering Jobs in Australia, along with the demand of a specific job, salary, qualification needed, and how to apply to them. Let us explore the exciting opportunities in most in-demand engineering fields in Australia.

List of Top 10 Engineering Jobs in Australia

Australia provides a best workplace for potential engineering professionals who possess various specializations in different industries and sectors. Let’s begin with our top 10 engineering jobs in Australia.

1. Civil Engineering

Civil Engineering is one of the most demanded engineering professions in Australia. As the population is growing higher continuously in Australia, the demand for civil engineers is growing higher as well. Currently, there are around 56,100 full-time civil engineers working in this field.

ANZSCO Code 233211
Role Being responsible for designing, constructing, Civil engineers are also required for maintaining infrastructure projects like roads, bridges, and buildings.
Average Salary in Australia As of 2023, the average salary of Civil Engineers is around AUD $91,000 per year.

How to Get a Civil Engineering Job in Australia?

Civil Engineering is a promising career in Australia with so many benefits. An individual can get Civil Engineering Job in Australia if he/she has:

  • Completed a Bachelor’s degree in Civil Engineering or a related field
  • Registered as a professional engineer with Engineers Australia
  • A minimum of Two years of work experience through internships or entry-level positions
  • Completed the National Engineering Registration Stage 1 Competency Standards
  • A language proficiency
  • Joined a professional job seeking platform like SEEK, LinkedIn and more.

2. Software Engineering

Software Engineering is a growing field in the Australian job market. For starters, they are the ones who play a crucial role in both the business and IT industry by developing innovative solutions for a wide range of challenges.

ANZSCO Code 233512
Role Software Engineers have a role to design, develop, and maintain software applications & systems. Different advanced softwares and applications that are needed in the industry are developed & maintained by software engineers.
Average Salary in Australia Software Engineering professionals in Australia earn an annual salary between AUD $82,000 to AUD $120,000.

How to Get a Software Engineering Job in Australia?

  • Must hold a degree in Computer Science or Software Engineering
  • A strong portfolio of coding and software development projects
  • Must have work experience working in entry-level jobs or junior positions for two or more years.
  • A language proficiency
  • Joined a professional job seeking platform like SEEK, LinkedIn and more.

3. Mechanical Engineering

Mechanical Engineering is one of the highest sought engineering disciplines in Australia. They are the ones who create machines that generate power, like electric generators, engines and turbines, and machines that consume power like AC and refrigeration systems.

ANZSCO Code 233512
Role Mechanical Engineers have a role of design and management of machinery, equipment, and mechanical systems. Their expertise found in industries encompasses manufacturing, automobiles, and aerospace.
Average Salary in Australia Mechanical Engineers earn an average salary ranging AUD $70,000 to AUD $120,000.

How to Get a Mechanical Engineering Job in Australia?

To get Mechanical Engineering Job in Australia, you must have:

  • A Bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering
  • Practical experience through internships or cooperative education programs
  • A language proficiency
  • Joined a professional job seeking platform like SEEK, LinkedIn and more.

4. Biomedical Engineering

Biomedical Engineering is an associative field that combines all three principles of biology, medicine, and engineering to design and develop different solutions for the healthcare system & medical industries. It also aims to improve the quality of the healthcare system, make better medical equipment to understand the human body and interactions with technology.

ANZSCO Code 233913
Role They play the key roles such as Medical device design & development, Research and innovation, and also contribute in entrepreneurship and industry.
Average Salary in Australia Biomedical Engineering professionals earn an average salary of AUD $97,000 per year.

How to Get a Biomedical Engineering Job in Australia?

To get a Biomedical Engineering Job in Australia, a candidate must have:

  • A bachelor or master’s degree in biomedicine with Bioengineering systems major
  • Hands-on experience through internships, projects, or part-time jobs
  • A language proficiency
  • Joined a professional job seeking platform like SEEK, LinkedIn and more.

5. Geotechnical Engineering

Geotechnical Engineering, though, is a branch of civil engineering but extremely important in the field of engineering. Geotechnical Engineering focuses on understanding the behavior of earth materials and their interaction with different projects, infrastructure, and the environment.

ANZSCO Code 233212
Role Their role is to deal with the analysis, design, and construction of structures and systems. Geotechnical Engineers are responsible to study and review all the natural environment of a specific place before any construction work takes place.
Average Salary in Australia Geotechnical Engineers earn an average salary of AUD $90,986 per year. The salary for Entry-level positions starts from AUD $67,000 to AUD $80,000 per year.

How to Get a Geotechnical Engineering Job in Australia?

To become a geotechnical engineer a candidate must have:

  • A Bachelor/ Master Degree in Engineering with a Major in Geotechnical Engineering or Civil engineering
  • Acquired theoretical knowledge with practical insights on soil testing and site investigation
  • A Minimum of two years of supervised work experience

6. Electrical Engineering

Electrical Engineering deals with the study, design, development, and application of electrical systems, devices and technologies. The electricity we all get in our homes and workplaces is all possible because of electrical engineering. This field covers a wide range of applications, including electronics, telecommunications, power generation, robotics and more.

ANZSCO Code 233311
Role The role of an electrical engineer in Australia is to design, develop, and test different electrical systems. Being an electrical engineer one has options to work in sectors such as construction, telecommunication, manufacturing, aerospace, and more.
Average Salary in Australia The average salary of an electrical engineer in Australia varies based on industry, location, and experience. According to Glassdoor, an electrical engineer earns an average salary of AUD $78,858 per year.

How to Get an Electrical Engineering Job in Australia?

To get electrical engineering job in Australia, a candidate must have:

  • A Bachelor/Master degree in electrical engineering
  • At Least one or two years of work experience
  • An Engineering Certification from a recognised authority ( E.g., Engineers Australia)
  • Familiarity with softwares like CAD, MATLAB, Python and more

7. Chemical Engineering

Chemical Engineering is another fascinating career that combines principles of chemistry, physics, mathematics, and engineering. It is needed to design, develop, and optimize processes for the production, transformation, and utilization of chemicals, materials, and energy.

ANZSCO Code 233111
Role They perform a fascinating role of combining chemistry, physics and math to design, develop, and optimize chemical processes, and create new materials.
Average Salary in Australia The average salary for a chemical engineer in Australia is around AUD $73,162 per year. Chemical Engineering is one of the highest paying engineering jobs in Australia.

How to Get a Chemical Engineering Job in Australia?

To get Chemical Engineering Job in Australia, a Candidate must have:

  • A 4-year undergraduate degree in chemical engineering or a closely related field like chemistry or physics
  • Applicable hands-on experience in the realm of chemical engineering or a closely related field
  • Official certification from either the Institution of Chemical Engineers or Engineers Australia

8. Agricultural Engineering

Agricultural Engineering applies engineering principles towards agricultural production and natural resource management. It is useful for improving agricultural processes, machinery, equipment, and systems to enhance productivity & efficiency in farming.

ANZSCO Code 233912
Role They are involved in designing irrigation systems, farm machinery, harvest handling equipment, renewable energy solutions for agriculture and handling water resource challenges.
Average Salary in Australia Agricultural Engineers have a starting salary of AUD $67,000 that typically reaches AUD $131,000 per year in Australia.

How to Get an Agricultural Engineering Job in Australia?

To get an Agricultural Engineering job in Australia, an applicant must have:

  • A Bachelor/Master degree in Agricultural Engineering or related field
  • knowledge via internships or farm related works

9. Industrial Engineering

Industrial Engineering is an engineering field that focuses on the design, analysis, and control maintenance of production and operational systems. Industrial Engineering is currently one of the highest paid and most demanded occupations in Australia.

ANZSCO Code 233511
Role Industrial engineers deal and integrate workers, machines, information and energy related works.
Average Salary in Australia The average salary for Industrial Engineers can range from AUD $82,125 to AUD $100,000 per year in Australia .

How to Get an Industrial Engineering Job in Australia?

To get an Industrial Engineering Job in Australia, an applicant must have:

  • Obtained a Bachelor’s degree in Industrial Engineering or a related field from a recognised college or university
  • Obtained relevant certifications from Australian Industrial Authority.
  • Obtained a basic training in Industry operations and field work
  • A English Proficiency such as IELTS

10. Petroleum Engineering

Petroleum Engineering is a branch of engineering that works on development and exploitation of crude oil and natural gas fields. It is also a scientific way to analyze, and forecast the oil production possibility and performance.

ANZSCO Code 233612
Role People who study Petroleum Engineering are called Petroleum Engineers. Petroleum Engineers play a vital role in the exploration, extraction, and production of oils and gas from different places..
Average Salary in Australia According to PayScale, the average salary of a Petroleum Engineer in Australia is AUD $136,500.

How to Get a Petroleum Engineering Job in Australia?

To get Petroleum Engineering Job in Australia, an applicant must have:

  • Obtained a Bachelor’s degree in Petroleum Engineering or a related field from a recognised college or university
  • Gained a specialized knowledge through internship or entry-level jobs
  • Obtained a basic training in oil extraction and exploration
  • A English Proficiency such as IELTS

How to Apply for Engineering Jobs in Australia?

If you want to get an engineering job in Australia, Just follow these 5 easy steps.

1. Check Your Eligibility

First, see if you're allowed to work in Australia. You can do this on the Australian Government's website. If you have completed your education there recently, think about the Temporary Graduate Visa. It lets you stay in the country and learn from working.

2. Look for Jobs

Find engineering jobs in Australia by using websites like:

  • LinkedIn
  • Indeed
  • Seek
  • Glassdoor

3. Fix Your Resume and Letter

Make your resume and cover letter special for the job. Talk about the skills and things you know that can help the company.

4. Apply for Jobs

When your resume and cover letter are good to go, send them in. Do this on job websites like LinkedIn, Indeed, and Glassdoor.

5. Get Ready for Interviews

If a company wants to talk to you, get ready! Learn about the company and the job. Practice answering questions they might ask in the interview.

Stick to these steps, and you'll be ready for an engineering job in Australia!
