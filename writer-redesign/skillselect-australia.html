What Is Skillselect Australia And Its Visa Categories?

What is SkillSelect in Australia and how does it work?

SkillSelect is an Australian immigration program that connects skilled international workers with Australian employers. It is an online application process that allows skilled workers and business people to migrate to Australia by submitting an Expression of Interest (EOI).

The system works in the same way as an online job board, matching employees to companies based on their skills and needs. Employers can evaluate the profiles of foreign workers and recommend that they apply for a visa after answering a series of questions about their background and skills.

Relation Between SkillSelect and EOI

An EOI is stored in the SkillSelect database for two years. The platform also allows you to save an incomplete EOI for later editing.

EOIs are typically updated in the following circumstances:

  • When you have a new work experience
  • When you have secured higher education
  • Improvement in English proficiency skills
  • Achieved new skill set
  • Changes in family e.g. you had a newborn

How Does SkillSelect Function?

  1. Applicants upload their information into SkillSelect using an online form provided by the Australian Department of Home Affairs (DHA).
  2. Applicants complete a talent exam to assess their abilities, English language knowledge, and educational level.
  3. Applicants must submit an Expression of Interest (EOI) in the visa category for which they wish to be considered.
  4. Once in the system, Australian businesses or state and territory governments may invite the applicant to apply for a visa. This is the nomination or sponsorship process necessary for most skilled worker visas.
  5. The applicant fills out a visa application.
  6. If the application is approved, they will grant the applicant a visa, and will live and work in Australia.

How SkillSelect works with Skilled Points Visas

To submit an EOI, you must have completed your skills assessment and provide proof of your English language proficiency.

You may also want to get a nomination from a state or territory government before submitting an EOI, as this will increase your chances of getting an invitation.

Types of Skilled Worker Visas

1. Skilled Independent Visa (Subclass 189)

This is a permanent resident visa for skilled employees who have passed a points exam. Skilled Independent 189 Visa is one of the most popular Visa categories for SkillSelect Australia. The processing time for a skilled independent visa 189 depends on a variety of factors. It can take anywhere from 8 months to a year. The skilled independent visa-subclass 189 has a base cost of AUD 4,115. In addition to this fee, there is an extra charge based on the applicants' age. It is AUD 1030 for applicants under the age of 18, and AUD 2055 for applicants over the age of 18.

Requirements while applying for Subclass 189:

  • Be nominated for a skilled occupation on the relevant skilled occupation list.
  • Have a suitable skills evaluation for that career.
  • Should not exceed the age of 50.
  • Based on the elements in the points test, you have attained the score given in your letter of invitation.
  • Fulfill the health & character requirements.

2. Skilled Nominated Visa (subclass 190)

This is a permanent resident visa for skilled employees who have passed a points exam. The time it takes to process an Australian visa is determined by the completion of all necessary steps. 75% of visa applications are processed within seven months, while 90% may take up to 9 months.

You need to meet the requirements mentioned below to apply for this visa:

  • Be nominated for a skilled occupation on the relevant skilled occupation list.
  • Have a suitable skills evaluation for that career.
  • Have not yet reached the age of 50.
  • Based on the elements in the points test, you have attained the score given in your letter of invitation.
  • You should be able to communicate in English effectively.
  • Have been nominated by a state or territory government agency in Australia.

3. Skilled Regional (Provisional) Visa (subclass 489)

This is a four-year visa that allows skilled workers to live and work in selected Australian regions. There are various requirements if you apply through the invited pathway or the extended stay pathway. The 489 visa is a four-year provisional visa. You should live in a regional area for two years and should be working full-time with a minimum of 35 hours a week before you apply for permanent residency.

Requirements:

  • Experienced in any of the professions listed in the list of Eligible Skilled Occupations
  • At least 65 point score based on the applicant's age, Qualification, and Experience
  • Get a state sponsorship
  • Should not exceed the age of 50
  • English Proficiency from IELTS
  • Experience, as required by state
  • Submit an Expression of Interest (EOI)
  • Meet health and character requirements
  • Live in sponsoring state for two years

4. Temporary Work (Skilled) Visa (subclass 457)

This skilled worker visa is valid for up to four years. It results in sponsorship and employment in a nominated occupation. This is one of the most popular visa categories for the SkillSelect program for applicants who want to work in Australia temporarily. The most common visa used to sponsor employees to work in Australia is the 457 visa. The employer can be an Australian or international company. This visa is no longer available for new applications and has been replaced by the TSS 482 visa.

Advantages of subclass 457:

  • stay in Australia for up to four years
  • work full-time
  • travel without restriction
  • bring eligible family members to Australia as dependents

5. Employer Nomination Scheme (subclass 186)

The Employer Nomination Scheme (Subclass 186) allows suitably skilled overseas workers to be sponsored by eligible Australian employers to live and work permanently in Australia. Before applying for this visa, applicants must be nominated by an Australian employer. If the applications aren't submitted, they must be submitted within 6 months of the nomination being approved. The visa will not be issued if the position is no longer open or the nomination has been withdrawn.

Requirements:

  • Should be nominated by a legitimate Australian employer
  • An IELTS (or equivalent test) score of 6 in each component
  • Direct Entry stream applicants will have to be at least 45 years old at the time of application
  • Applicants for the Temporary Residence Transition stream will still be required to be 50 or older at the time of application
  • Apply in the category for which you were nominated (the Temporary Residence Transition Stream, the Direct Entry stream, or the Agreement stream).

6. Regional Sponsored Migration Scheme Visa (subclass 187)

For skilled foreign workers with a nominating employer in an Australian regional or low population growth area, the 187 visa is a permanent visa. This visa enables skilled workers to live and work permanently in Australia after being nominated by their employers in regional Australia. You must be under 45 years of age, meet the required skills, qualifications, and English language requirements, and have a job offer from an authorized Australian employer for a position in a regional Australia in order to be eligible to apply for this visa.

  • Should be nominated for a job in regional Australia by an approved Australian employer
  • Must be under the age of 50
  • Meet the skills, qualifications, and English language criteria
  • Apply in the category for which you were nominated

7. Business Talent (Permanent) Visa (subclass 132)

This visa category is different compared to other categories for the SkillSelect Australia program. This is an entrepreneur visa that permits you to start a new business or expand an existing one in Australia. To be eligible for this visa, you must be nominated by a state or territory government agency and asked to apply by the minister.

You must also have one of the following:

  • If you wish to apply for the visa in the Significant Business History stream, you must have at least AUD 1.5 million in net business and personal assets and an annual business turnover of at least AUD 3 million.
  • If you plan to apply for a visa in the Venture Capital Entrepreneur stream, you must have received at least AUD 1 million in venture capital funding to begin the commercialization and development of a high-value business idea in Australia.

8. Business Innovation and Investment (Provisional) Visa (subclass 188)

This is a temporary visa for entrepreneurs who have experience running profitable businesses and who want to start, run, or expand an inventive business in Victoria.

Who may submit a visa application?

  • Successful business owners who want to operate in South Australia make up the business innovation stream.
  • Successful business owners or investors who are prepared to put at least AUD$2.5 million (or AUD$3.75 million if they are over 55 years old) into a compliant investment fund over the course of the provisional visa are eligible for the investor stream.
  • People willing to put $5 million into a compliant investment fund for the duration of the provisional visa are considered significant investors.
