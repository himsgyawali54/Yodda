Skill Assessment Validity for Engineers Australia

Are you an engineer planning to migrate to Australia? If so, you've probably heard about the Skill Assessment process conducted by Engineers Australia.

In this blog post, we'll delve into Skill Assessment validity for Engineers Australia, breaking it down into simple terms. Whether you're a fresh graduate or an experienced professional, understanding the validity of your Skill Assessment is essential for a successful migration process. We'll explore the key aspects of Skill Assessment validity, including the duration, renewal requirements, and impact on your migration plans.

What is Skill Assessment?

Skill Assessment for Engineers Australia is a process designed to evaluate the qualifications and experience of engineers who want to migrate to Australia. It helps determine if you have the necessary skills and knowledge to work as an engineer in Australia. The assessment examines your education, work history, and other relevant documents to ensure they meet Australia's standards. It's like a thorough check to ensure that you have the right expertise and abilities to work as an engineer in Australia. Once you pass the assessment, you receive formal recognition of your qualifications, which is important for finding engineering jobs in Australia.

Preparing for Engineers Australia Assessment: Key Steps

Assessing Your Engineering Qualification: Pathways to Consider When applying for an Engineers Australia assessment, evaluating your engineering qualification for migration is crucial. This begins by selecting the appropriate assessment pathway. Here are the pathways available to choose from:

1. Accredited Qualifications

  • Australian Qualifications
  • Washington Accord Qualifications
  • Sydney Accord Qualifications
  • Dublin Accord Qualifications
  • Other Recognized Qualifications

2. Non-Accredited Qualifications

  • Competency Demonstration Report (CDR)

A. English Language Competency

Engineers Australia requires applicants to provide evidence of English language proficiency as part of the assessment process. This ensures that you have the necessary language skills for effective communication in an engineering context. Here's what you need to know:

English Test Results: Accepted Tests and Submission

Engineers Australia currently accepts three English language tests as valid evidence: IELTS, TOEFL iBT®, and PTE ACADEMIC. If you opt for the PTE Academic test, send your score to "Engineers Australia" using Pearson's online secure site for verification.

Validity Period of English Test Results

Effective from March 1, 2021, Engineers Australia accepts IELTS, TOEFL iBT, and PTE Academic English test scores for up to three years from the test date at the time of receiving your application. It's essential to keep this timeframe in mind while planning your application.

B. Assessment for Limited Engineering Experience

Even if you have limited engineering experience, you can still apply for an assessment of your engineering qualification. Work experience is optional for the assessment. You can showcase your abilities by referencing your undergraduate engineering degree and highlighting project or work experience components from your academic studies.

C. Verifying Accreditation of Your Qualification

You can use the International Engineering Alliance (IEA) website Qualification Checker to determine if your qualification is accredited. This tool helps you verify if the representative body of the signatory country has accredited your qualification.

Important Note:
If you apply through the Accredited Qualifications pathway, you must ensure your qualification is from an accredited program. Please note that if your qualification is later found unaccredited, no refund will be provided.

Document Requirements, Steps and Eligibility Requirements To Apply For The Skills Assessment EA

Eligibility Requirements To Apply For The Skills Assessment EA

Education and Work Experience

It would be best to hold a relevant engineering degree recognized by a professional body or authority from a recognized institution. Additionally, you must have at least two years of verified work experience as an engineer, supported by professional references.

English Language Proficiency

You need to demonstrate proficiency in English by taking an approved language test such as IELTS or TOEFL, achieving a score that meets the criteria set by the respective country.

Professional Registration

It is essential to be registered as a professional engineer with the relevant professional body in your own country.

Character and Health Requirements

You must meet the character and health requirements specified by your country, including providing a police report and undergoing a medical examination.

Document Checklist for Accredited Qualification Pathways

Make sure you have the following documents ready for your application:

  • Recent passport-size photo
  • Passport
  • Curriculum Vitae
  • Official academic transcript
  • Academic degree qualification
  • Any name change document
  • English language test results (IELTS, TOEFL)
  • Other specific requirements based on the pathway chosen

Document Checklist for Non-Accredited Qualification Pathways

Prepare the following documents for your application:

  • Recent passport-size photo
  • Passport
  • Curriculum Vitae (CV)
  • Official academic transcript
  • Academic degree qualification
  • Any name change document
  • English language test results (IELTS, TOEFL)
  • Documentary evidence of employment (employment reference letter and payslips)
  • Relevant registration certificate
  • List of Continuing Professional Development (CPD)
  • Summary Statement
  • Three Career Episodes

Steps To Follow For The EA Skills Assessment

1. Prepare and Submit Your Application

To prepare your application, follow the guidelines in the "Prepare your migration skills assessment application" link. Once your application is ready, create an account on the Engineers Australia portal. Login with your account ID, select the migration skills assessment, upload all the required documents, and submit your application. You'll receive an email confirmation upon successful submission.

2. EA Skill Assessment Processing Time

The processing time for the Migration Skills Assessment application typically ranges from 8 to 12 weeks. The duration depends on the quality of your documents and any necessary corrections. You can check the status of your application by logging into your account on the EA portal or contacting us via email.

3. Verify Your Skill Assessment Outcome

To verify your EA Skill Assessment Outcome, log in to the Engineers Australia portal using your last name, EA ID, and Application ID. If applicable, provide the Department of Home Affairs Reference (for Department of Home Affairs Staff Members) and access the outcome of your assessment.

4. Receive your Assessment Result

Upon receiving your application decision, you will be notified via email. If your application is successful, you will receive a Positive Migration Skills Assessment Outcome letter, which can be used for your migration purposes. This assessment letter remains valid indefinitely with Engineers Australia. However, please check the Department of Home Affairs website for the valid period for migration visa applications. For visa-related inquiries, contacting the Department of Home Affairs is advisable. Please feel free to contact us for more detailed information regarding this topic.

Validity of Engineers Australia Skill Assessment: Renewal Process

Duration of Australian Skills Assessment Validity

When it comes to the validity of an Australian skills assessment, it remains valid for three years. If your skills assessment letter does not mention a specific date, you can consider it valid for three years from the issue date. However, if the assessment letter bears a date less than three years old, the validity period follows the date stated in the letter.

When the skills assessment letter has a three-year validity period, the Department will consider only the most recent date for assessment purposes.

Renewing Your Engineering Skills Assessment in Australia

If your TRA (Trade Recognition Australia) Skills Assessment Outcome Letter has expired, it cannot be renewed. Instead, you will need to apply for a new skills assessment and go through the application process again. This means submitting a new application, providing updated documentation, and undergoing the assessment process anew.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

The validity of a skills assessment for Australia typically lasts for three years. However, if the letter mentions a date within the last three years, the validity follows that specific date. It's important to note that the Department will consider the most recent date if multiple assessments have been issued.

Unfortunately, skill assessments with Engineers Australia cannot be renewed once they expire. If your skill assessment outcome letter has expired, you must apply for a new skills assessment and go through the entire assessment process again.
